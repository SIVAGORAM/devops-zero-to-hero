# Day 3: Software Roles & Agile - Interview Questions

These questions test your understanding of how a real-world enterprise software team operates, proving you have practical industry knowledge beyond just technical tools.

---

### Q1: What is the primary role of a DevOps Engineer in a product team?
**Interview Answer:**  
"A DevOps Engineer acts as the bridge between Development and Operations. While developers write the core application logic, my role is to automate the building, testing, deployment, and infrastructure management. I ensure that the software can be released rapidly, securely, and reliably without manual bottlenecks."

---

### Q2: What is the difference between a Product Manager and a Product Owner?
**Interview Answer:**  
"The Product Manager is focused on the high-level vision and strategy-they decide *what* we are building and *why*. The Product Owner is focused on execution-they take that vision, break it down into actionable user stories, and manage the team's backlog to dictate exactly *how* and *when* the development team builds it."

---

### Q3: How do you receive and track your daily tasks as a DevOps Engineer?
**Interview Answer:**  
"In a professional Agile environment, work is tracked using project management tools like Jira. The Product Owner creates Epics and breaks them down into User Stories and technical Tasks. During Sprint Planning, DevOps-specific tasks-like provisioning a new AWS environment or writing a CI/CD pipeline-are assigned to me with estimated completion times."

---

### Q4: What is a Sprint and how does it fit into Agile?
**Interview Answer:**  
"A Sprint is a fixed development cycle, usually lasting two weeks, used in the Scrum framework. Instead of waiting months to release a massive update, the team commits to a small chunk of work and delivers a working, shippable piece of software at the end of every sprint. This allows us to move fast and adapt to customer feedback continuously."

---

### Q5: What is the purpose of a Sprint Retrospective?
**Interview Answer:**  
"A Sprint Retrospective is a meeting held at the end of every sprint where the entire team reviews the process. We discuss what went well, what failed, and what can be improved for the next sprint. It's a critical part of the DevOps culture because it fosters continuous improvement and blameless problem-solving."

---

### Q6: What is a Daily Standup?
**Interview Answer:**  
"A Daily Standup is a short, 15-minute Agile meeting held every morning. Every team member answers three core questions: What did I accomplish yesterday? What am I working on today? And are there any blockers preventing me from completing my work? As a DevOps engineer, this is where I communicate if infrastructure or pipeline issues are blocking developers."

---

### Q7: Why do DevOps teams often prefer Kanban over Scrum?
**Interview Answer:**  
"While development teams thrive on Scrum's fixed 2-week sprints to build features, DevOps and SRE work is often unpredictable. Server outages, security patches, and pipeline failures cannot wait for the next sprint planning session. Kanban provides a continuous flow model without fixed timeboxes, which is far better suited for the rapid, reactive nature of operational work."

---

### Q8: What happens in a Sprint Review vs Backlog Refinement?
**Interview Answer:**  
"A Sprint Review happens at the end of the sprint where the team demonstrates the working software we just built to the stakeholders. Backlog Refinement, on the other hand, happens during the sprint to review and clarify future user stories so they are ready for the *next* sprint planning session."
